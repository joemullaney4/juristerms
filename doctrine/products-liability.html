Products Liability

Course: Torts

Products Liability is a legal doctrine or concept commonly studied in Torts and tested in law school courses, bar exam preparation, and legal analysis.

In plain English, Products Liability refers to a rule, principle, test, or framework that courts use when deciding certain legal questions. Understanding the meaning of Products Liability helps students recognize how lawyers and judges organize legal arguments.

The doctrine matters because it gives courts a predictable way to evaluate disputes. When a legal issue falls within the scope of Products Liability, the doctrine can shape how a court interprets facts, applies precedent, and reaches a conclusion.

Law students often encounter Products Liability in casebooks, outlines, class discussions, and exam hypos. A strong working understanding of Products Liability makes it easier to identify issues, articulate rules, and apply doctrine in a structured way.
