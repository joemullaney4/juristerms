Duty of Good Faith

Course: Corporations

Duty of Good Faith is a legal doctrine or concept commonly studied in Corporations and tested in law school courses, bar exam preparation, and legal analysis.

In plain English, Duty of Good Faith refers to a rule, principle, test, or framework that courts use when deciding certain legal questions. Understanding the meaning of Duty of Good Faith helps students recognize how lawyers and judges organize legal arguments.

The doctrine matters because it gives courts a predictable way to evaluate disputes. When a legal issue falls within the scope of Duty of Good Faith, the doctrine can shape how a court interprets facts, applies precedent, and reaches a conclusion.

Law students often encounter Duty of Good Faith in casebooks, outlines, class discussions, and exam hypos. A strong working understanding of Duty of Good Faith makes it easier to identify issues, articulate rules, and apply doctrine in a structured way.
