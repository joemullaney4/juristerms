Duty to Mitigate

Course: Contracts

Duty to Mitigate is a legal doctrine or concept commonly studied in Contracts and tested in law school courses, bar exam preparation, and legal analysis.

In plain English, Duty to Mitigate refers to a rule, principle, test, or framework that courts use when deciding certain legal questions. Understanding the meaning of Duty to Mitigate helps students recognize how lawyers and judges organize legal arguments.

The doctrine matters because it gives courts a predictable way to evaluate disputes. When a legal issue falls within the scope of Duty to Mitigate, the doctrine can shape how a court interprets facts, applies precedent, and reaches a conclusion.

Law students often encounter Duty to Mitigate in casebooks, outlines, class discussions, and exam hypos. A strong working understanding of Duty to Mitigate makes it easier to identify issues, articulate rules, and apply doctrine in a structured way.
