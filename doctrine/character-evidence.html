Character Evidence

Course: Evidence

Character Evidence is a legal doctrine or concept commonly studied in Evidence and tested in law school courses, bar exam preparation, and legal analysis.

In plain English, Character Evidence refers to a rule, principle, test, or framework that courts use when deciding certain legal questions. Understanding the meaning of Character Evidence helps students recognize how lawyers and judges organize legal arguments.

The doctrine matters because it gives courts a predictable way to evaluate disputes. When a legal issue falls within the scope of Character Evidence, the doctrine can shape how a court interprets facts, applies precedent, and reaches a conclusion.

Law students often encounter Character Evidence in casebooks, outlines, class discussions, and exam hypos. A strong working understanding of Character Evidence makes it easier to identify issues, articulate rules, and apply doctrine in a structured way.
