Nondelegation Doctrine

Course: Administrative Law

Nondelegation Doctrine is a legal doctrine or concept commonly studied in Administrative Law and tested in law school courses, bar exam preparation, and legal analysis.

In plain English, Nondelegation Doctrine refers to a rule, principle, test, or framework that courts use when deciding certain legal questions. Understanding the meaning of Nondelegation Doctrine helps students recognize how lawyers and judges organize legal arguments.

The doctrine matters because it gives courts a predictable way to evaluate disputes. When a legal issue falls within the scope of Nondelegation Doctrine, the doctrine can shape how a court interprets facts, applies precedent, and reaches a conclusion.

Law students often encounter Nondelegation Doctrine in casebooks, outlines, class discussions, and exam hypos. A strong working understanding of Nondelegation Doctrine makes it easier to identify issues, articulate rules, and apply doctrine in a structured way.
